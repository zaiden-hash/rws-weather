SHORT TERM (Tonight through Sunday): A fast-moving shortwave trough will clip the region late this afternoon, bringing a brief period of light snow showers primarily across the higher terrain before 4 PM. Temperatures will drop through the 30s overnight with northwest winds gusting to near 30 mph. Wind chills as low as 20°F are possible by early Sunday morning.

SUNDAY: High pressure builds from the west, bringing a pleasant partly sunny day with temperatures rebounding into the lower 50s. South winds at 8 to 14 mph will moderate through the afternoon. Skies clear overnight as the high shifts offshore.

LONG TERM (Monday onward): Above-normal temperatures expected Monday as a strong southerly flow develops ahead of the next approaching system. Highs reach the low 60s, near 15 degrees above the seasonal average for late March. A cold front will arrive by Tuesday, bringing the chance of showers. Temperatures return to seasonable values for mid-week.

Aviation: VFR conditions expected through the period. Isolated MVFR ceilings possible Saturday evening across northern stations during the snow shower activity. Winds remain gusty Saturday, diminishing overnight.
